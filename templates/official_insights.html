Ergot Disease Survey in Pearl Millet - Marathwada Region

A comprehensive survey across nine tehsils in Chh. Sambhaji Nagar district (Kharif 2023-24) assessed ergot disease prevalence. Disease incidence ranged from 5.50% (Phulambari) to 22.00% (Kannad), with a mean of 13.19%. Among cultivars, local varieties showed highest susceptibility (16.33%), while Pioneer 86M38 demonstrated best resistance (8.04%).

Ergot Disease Management Guide

ICRISAT's foundational manual on pearl millet ergot covers disease distribution, symptoms, fungal morphology, and disease cycle. Grain yield losses range from 58-70% under favorable conditions. The guide emphasizes host-plant resistance as the primary control strategy.

Pathogen Biology 📅 Recent 🌍 Africa & Asia

Understanding Claviceps fusiformis

This specialized fungal pathogen affects pearl millet through honeydew production (creamy to pink droplets) on infected florets. Dark sclerotia replace grains, making produce unsafe due to toxic alkaloid content.

Biology and Genetics of Ergot in Cereals

Review of ergot diseases in cereals, focusing on Claviceps fusiformis in pearl millet. The pathogen infects unfertilized ovaries. A1 cytoplasm in male-sterile hybrids increases ergot severity, though nuclear genetic factors also influence resistance.

Disease Incidence Across Pearl Millet Varieties

Integrated Control Strategies

Effective ergot management combines host-plant resistance, cultural practices, and timely interventions. Resistant cultivars offer the most economical and sustainable control. Screening focuses on nuclear genetic factors for durable protection.

Geographic Distribution 📅 Current Status 🗺️ Regional Impact

Global Distribution and Economic Impact

Claviceps fusiformis threatens pearl millet across Africa and Asia, particularly F1 hybrids in India. Toxic sclerotia cause yield losses and render produce unsafe for consumption, creating food security concerns in affected regions.
